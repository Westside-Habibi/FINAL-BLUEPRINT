TOPIC 01
The Decision
I Have Made a Decision — August 1st I'm Moving Out
This is not a maybe. You have a plan, a date, a location, and a landlord who can help. You need your therapist to know this is happening so they can help you execute it — not keep processing whether you should.
Say This
"I've made my decision. I'm moving out August 1st. I have a plan — VASH benefit, a landlord who is also a realtor, a coastal 3-bedroom in the Huntington/Newport/Costa Mesa area. I don't need help deciding anymore. I need help getting through the next 6 weeks without falling back into old patterns."
TOPIC 02
The Anxiety
I'm Anxious About Nidia's Reaction — And I Already Know What It Will Be
You know she'll be passive aggressive. You know the cycle — she fights it, then becomes the person you fell in love with to win you back. Then once she has you, complacency returns. The anxiety isn't about the unknown. It's about holding firm when she becomes that person again.
Say This
"My anxiety isn't about what she'll say. I already know — she'll be passive aggressive at first. What I'm scared of is when she shifts and becomes the woman I fell in love with to pull me back. That's happened every time before. I need tools to stay the course when that happens — because that's the moment I've always broken."
TOPIC 03
The Pattern
She Changes to Win — Then Gets Complacent Once She Has Me Back
This is the cycle that has kept you trapped. She doesn't change because she's grown — she changes because it's a competition and you're the prize. Once she wins, the growth stops. You need your therapist to help you name this clearly so when it shows up again you can see it for what it is.
Say This
"There's a pattern I need help with. Every time I've pulled away or left, she becomes the woman I originally fell in love with. Engaged, present, loving. And I come back. Then within months she's complacent again and I'm invisible. I need to understand why I keep responding to that — and how to stop."
TOPIC 04
The Fear
I'm Scared I'll End Up Alone
Even with clarity about what needs to happen, there's a quiet fear underneath that you'll leave and end up with nothing. This fear is what keeps people in situations they've outgrown. It needs to be spoken out loud — not managed around.
Say This
"Underneath all of this there's a fear I haven't said out loud much — that I'll leave and end up alone. That I'll lose everything familiar and have nothing. I know logically that's not true but it's there and it's been part of why I've stayed. I want to work through where that fear comes from."
TOPIC 05
Giselle
There Is Someone Else — And I Still Love Her
In 2018 you met someone named Giselle who showed you what real love feels like. Nidia broke that up through guilt. Giselle is currently in a relationship but a month ago you both confirmed the feelings are still mutual. You're afraid you missed your chance. Your therapist needs the full picture.
Say This
"There's someone I need to talk about. In 2018 I met a woman named Giselle who showed me what love is supposed to feel like. Nidia pulled me back through guilt and I lost that relationship. Giselle and I still have feelings for each other — we confirmed that recently. She's in a relationship now and I'm scared I've missed my chance. This isn't why I'm leaving — but I need to work through the guilt and fear around it."
TOPIC 06
The Conversation
Help Me Prepare For The Conversation With Nidia
You need practical tools — not just emotional support. How to stay calm when she escalates. What to do when she cries or gets passive aggressive. How to not get pulled into defending your decision. Ask to role play it.
Say This
"I need practical help preparing for the conversation with Nidia. Can we actually role play it? I want to practice staying calm when she escalates, not defending my decision over and over, and knowing when to end the conversation if it goes in circles. I know what I want to say — I just need to practice holding my ground when it gets hard."
TOPIC 07
The Pattern
The Emails Are Not The Issue — Feeling Unheard Is
She didn't respond to two emails about something that directly affects her wellbeing. But this isn't just about the emails. This is a pattern spanning the entire marriage — years of feeling invisible, unheard, like your words and feelings don't matter to her. The emails are just the latest version of something you've felt the whole time. This pattern is what makes you feel disrespected — and it needs to be named clearly so you stop internalizing it as your fault.
Say This
"I sent two emails to Nidia about something important and she didn't respond to either. It bothers me more than it should — but I realize it's not really about the emails. It's a pattern I've felt the whole marriage. Years of my words not mattering, my feelings going unheard, feeling invisible. I want to work on tools so I stop letting her silence have so much power over how I feel — because this has happened so many times and it always gets to me."

Just As Important
What NOT To Do In This Session
Don't let the session become about whether you should leave.
That decision is made. If the conversation drifts there — redirect: "I'm not here to decide. I'm here to prepare."
Don't spend the whole session on Nidia's feelings.
This session is about yours. Her reaction is hers to manage. Your job is staying grounded.
Don't leave without one specific tool.
Something concrete you can use when you feel yourself wavering — a phrase, an anchor, a physical reminder. Ask for it directly.
